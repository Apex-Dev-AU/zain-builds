## What it is

Zen Captures is my photography and videography brand. A project I've refined for enjoyment over time. 

The client-facing version lives at [zencaptures.com.au](https://zencaptures.com.au). This page is the quieter version of the story — how it started, how the brand changed, what I learned through the cameras, the website, the shoots, and the years of slowly taking it more seriously.

It is not just a photography business to me. It is one of the first projects where I kept coming back, rebuilding, refining, learning and trying to make the work feel more like what I had in my head.

## How it started

The earliest memory I have of properly using a camera goes back to around 2015.

At the start, it was not a business. It was just curiosity. Taking photos, testing angles, learning how light changed a moment, and realising that a camera could make ordinary things feel important.

The first time it felt like something more serious was an event I photographed when I was younger, around 16. It was a birthday event at a function centre — I do not remember every exact detail now, and I am not going to pretend I do — but I remember the feeling of being trusted to capture something for someone else.

That was different.

It was no longer just me taking photos for fun. There was a responsibility behind it. People were dressed up, families were together, moments were happening once, and the camera had to be ready.

The camera at the time was a **Sony A6000**. Looking back, it was a perfect first camera. Small, capable, affordable enough to learn on, but strong enough to take seriously. A lot of the basics came from that camera — framing, timing, light, editing, and learning that good photos are not just about settings.

## The gear changed slowly

The upgrade path happened over years.

- **Sony A6000** — the start.
- **Sony A7R II** — the move into full-frame and more serious image quality.
- **Sony A7R IV** — the current body.
- **Sony GM lenses** — the lens system the work eventually moved toward.
- **Sony 24–70mm GM f/2.8** — the main lens that has carried a lot of recent work.

The gear mattered, but it was not the whole story.

Better gear gave the work more room. More resolution, better files, stronger low-light performance, more confidence on serious jobs. But the actual improvement came from repetition — shooting events, learning how people move, understanding when to step in and when to disappear, and slowly building taste.

That part takes longer than buying a camera.

## How the brand changed

Zen Captures has been rebuilt more times than I can count.

The website changed.  
The logo changed.  
The wording changed.  
The packages changed.  
The way I described the work changed.

At different points, I was trying to make the brand look more premium, more polished, more professional, more refined. Some of that was useful. Some of it was probably overthinking. But each version taught me something about positioning, trust, SEO, storytelling, and how a creative business should present itself.

The current direction is much clearer.

Zen Captures is about capturing the moment properly — not just documenting that an event happened, but preserving the feeling of it. The goal is to build it into one of the most remarkable photo and cinema brands for events, weddings and proposals.

That is the long-term direction.

## What I have learned from it

Zen Captures taught me that creative work is not only about creativity.

It is also about systems.

How you speak to people.  
How you prepare before a shoot.  
How you organise files.  
How you deliver work.  
How you edit consistently.  
How your website explains the value before you ever speak to a client.  
How the brand feels before someone even sees the full gallery.

The photography is the visible part. The business underneath it is what makes the work sustainable.

That is what I am still learning.
