The Tiguan has been with me [ADD HOW LONG]. Long enough to
stop being a car I drive and start being a car I read about.

The reason it&rsquo;s interesting &mdash; more interesting
than the family-SUV exterior suggests &mdash; is the platform
underneath. It&rsquo;s the same MQB chassis that sits under
the Mk7 Golf GTI and Golf R. The same EA888 Gen3 engine.
The same DQ381 7-speed wet DSG. From factory it makes 162kW
through an IS20 turbo &mdash; about midway between a GTI and
a Golf R on paper, and not far off in practice, given the
weight and the all-wheel drive.

That&rsquo;s the technical version. The personal version is
that I bought a daily and ended up with a project car.

## The questions I&rsquo;m starting from

A list of things I want to understand, not in any order:

- How the IS20 actually works under load &mdash; where it&rsquo;s
  efficient, where it falls off, what the realistic ceiling
  is before you start trading reliability for power.
- What an IS38 swap actually involves on this chassis vs. on
  a Golf R, where it&rsquo;s a much more documented path.
- Whether a **Garrett Powermax** is a sensible next step for
  someone who values daily-driver reliability over peak
  numbers, or whether it&rsquo;s overkill for the use case.
- How much real-world difference a 3-inch downpipe and a
  bigger intercooler make to the IS20 before any tune.
- What suspension changes preserve the **DCC** adaptive
  damping modes &mdash; coilovers that talk to the factory
  module, not against it.
- How to service the DSG myself, properly, on a routine
  shorter than the factory&rsquo;s.
- How to read and clear codes confidently with OBD11, and
  what the warning signs are when a code is a symptom of
  something deeper.

The shortlist for the next month: the DSG service kit,
properly understanding what the existing fault codes mean
(there&rsquo;s a camshaft timing-related one I want to
trace down), and a clean baseline log of the car as it
sits.

## The point of writing this down

I&rsquo;m writing this because I&rsquo;ve noticed that
every time I learn something about the car I forget half
of it within a week. The mechanics happen on weekends; the
forgetting happens on weekdays. So this build log exists
to keep what I learn, in roughly the order I learn it.

It&rsquo;s also a deliberate counter-direction to the
car-content I usually see online &mdash; the rolling-shot
montage, the &ldquo;here&rsquo;s my new mod, smash that
subscribe button&rdquo; cadence. None of which actually
tells you anything useful about how a platform works.

I&rsquo;d rather have an honest paragraph than a slick
video.
