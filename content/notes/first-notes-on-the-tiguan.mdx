The Tiguan came home in November. I bought it as a daily and a
road-trip car. It has slowly become the thing I learn cars on.

The reason it&rsquo;s interesting is the platform underneath. It&rsquo;s
the same MQB chassis as the Mk7 Golf GTI and Golf R, the same EA888
Gen 3 2.0T, the same DQ500 wet DSG, Haldex AWD and DCC suspension. From
the outside it looks like a family SUV. Underneath it&rsquo;s the same
kit a Golf R runs.

## The direction

A lot of my early thinking on the car got stuck on power.

For a while I was looking at the bigger road: a Garrett PowerMax 2260S
turbo, a MODE intercooler, a 200-cell catted downpipe, serious tuning.
The numbers looked great. The more I sat with it, the more it felt
wrong for the car I actually drive. Most of my time in it is highway
and long road trips. Adding that much power would change the weight
transfer, the balance, the refinement &mdash; the parts of the car I
enjoy most.

So the build sits closer to **OEM+** now. Refined turbo character.
Hidden aggression. Chassis feel over dyno numbers.

The line I keep coming back to is: a premium executive Tiguan
that&rsquo;s secretly angry under boost. Not a race car. Not a show
car.

## What changed first

The first thing that actually changed how the car felt wasn&rsquo;t
power. It was adjustable sway bar links and a set of Continental tyres
up front.

Before the change the car had a slight floatiness at speed. I&rsquo;d
written it off as DCC Comfort mode. After the links and tyres it sat
flatter, lane changes felt more confident, and the cabin got quieter
on rough surfaces. The same car, more composed.

That reframed the whole direction. The car was telling me it wanted
refinement before it wanted power.

## What this build log is

I&rsquo;m writing this down because I forget. Things get learned on
weekends and lost by the following week. I&rsquo;d rather have an
honest set of notes than nothing.

It&rsquo;s also a counter to the kind of car content I usually see
&mdash; the rolling-shot montage, the &ldquo;here&rsquo;s my new
mod&rdquo; clip &mdash; which rarely tells you anything useful about
how a platform actually works. I&rsquo;d rather write a paragraph that
does.

The next few entries will be on specific pieces: the intake, the DSG
service, the brake refresh, the sway bar links, and why I stepped back
from the Garrett path.
