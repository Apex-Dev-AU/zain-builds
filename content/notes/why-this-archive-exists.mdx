This site isn&rsquo;t a portfolio. It&rsquo;s a working archive.

The difference matters to me. A portfolio is a sales surface. Its job
is to make the work look good for someone you want to be hired by.
Everything inside it has to be finished, photogenic and on its best
behaviour.

An archive is the opposite. Its job is to keep an honest record of
what&rsquo;s being made and learned over time, including the parts that
are messy, in progress, or quietly being rethought. Some entries are
polished. Most are working notes left in the open.

The reason I wanted a place like this is simple: I keep forgetting what
I&rsquo;ve learned. Things get figured out on weekends and slip away by
the following weekend. A few months later, the lesson is gone but the
problem comes back. Writing it down &mdash; even badly, even once
&mdash; keeps the lesson around long enough for the next attempt to
build on it.

The other reason is compounding. Individual notes aren&rsquo;t
particularly interesting. A few years of them, on the same set of
projects, start to add up to something. The Tiguan build, the AI
website experiments, the longer story of Zen Captures &mdash;
they&rsquo;re all the kind of work that gets more interesting in
hindsight than it is in the moment.

So this is the place. Build logs, project pages, short notes, a `/now`
that gets updated when the direction shifts. Updated when there&rsquo;s
something worth saying. Quiet when there isn&rsquo;t.
